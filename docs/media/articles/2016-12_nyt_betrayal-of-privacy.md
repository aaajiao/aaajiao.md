## Summary

This article explores how contemporary artists are creating work that reflects the dark side of our digital footprint and the erosion of privacy in the age of surveillance. The piece discusses an exhibition called "The Glass Room" organized by the Tactical Technology Collective in Berlin, which took over a gallery in Lower Manhattan to educate visitors about privacy and data security through art and interactive displays.

The article highlights several artists and works addressing themes of digital surveillance and privacy:

- **Aram Bartholl**: Created a book containing five million passwords stolen from LinkedIn in 2012
- **Mimi Onuoha**: "The Library of Missing Data Sets" - a filing cabinet with information not readily available online
- **Fidia Falaschetti**: "Social Security Cameras" - security cameras with casings replaced by logos of Twitter, Google, Snapchat, and Instagram
- **Aaajiao**: A Shanghai-based artist who created a machine printing lists of websites blocked by China's firewall
- **Sondra Perry**: "Resident Evil" at the Kitchen in Chelsea, mixing video-game perspectives with bodycam and news footage

The article notes how artists' approach to technology has shifted from the late 2000s' utopian view to a more critical examination of surveillance, corporate power, and data collection.
