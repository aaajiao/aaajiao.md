## 一、Summary and Motivation（摘要与动机）

**限制：最多1,000字符（含空格）**

### V4 Oracle 修订版

```
I am aaajiao (Xu Wenkai), a media artist working between Berlin and Shanghai. Since 2007 my practice has tracked how identity is governed by networks—from User to Bot to Player to "Void-Seeker," searching for cracks in algorithmic systems where resistance can circulate.

In parallel, my work is grounded in manufacturing. For ten years I have collaborated with craftsmen in a Shanghai acrylic factory; for the past five years my studio has been inside this factory. This symbiosis informs "Symbiosis," reading China's industrial shift through Absorption (入神)—meditative craft immersion—and Trance (出神)—a club-raised generation now spiritually adrift.

An exhibition at O—Overgaden offers a critical European vantage: as debates on trade and the human cost of "cheap goods" intensify, Copenhagen provides a precise context to examine these entangled flows between production and consumption.
```

## 二、Practice Description（实践描述）

**限制：最多3,000字符（含空格）**

### V4 Oracle 修订版

```
aaajiao is my working name and a strategy for visibility inside the attention economy. Since 2007 I have used networked images, games, sculpture, and documentary to examine algorithmic governance—how platforms shape subjectivity, and how "amnesia and aphasia" emerge when daily life is mediated by censorship, metrics, and recommendation.

Across the last decade my work has moved through a sequence of roles: User → Bot → Player → Void-Seeker. The User is locked into interfaces and infrastructure; the Bot adopts the machine's gaze; the Player tests agency through rule-based worlds (e.g., Deep Simulator); the Void-Seeker searches for an "Internet Void," fissures where empathy and refusal can pass through systems that try to totalize us.

Recent works develop this on two intertwined fronts.

Digital politics:
• Pinky Swear (2025) maps the emotional ecology of cyber-nationalism and the echo chambers of the Simplified Chinese internet.
• Guard, I… (2024–25) is a hyperreal silicone self-portrait as a sleeping security guard—an image of governance at the human scale, where sleep becomes a biological glitch.
• Minority Algorithm examines platform-induced "amnesia and aphasia" as a social pathology.

Material politics:
Symbiosis (2015–present) follows China's manufacturing transformation through a ten-year collaboration with acrylic-factory craftsmen who fabricate my works; for the past five years my Shanghai studio has been located inside their factory. The project documents two states: Absorption (入神), the worker's concentrated, almost meditative craft-flow; and Trance (出神), a generation shaped by club culture and now drifting through rapid social change. Alongside the factory, I filmed the final nights of a legendary Shanghai club before its closure.

The project combines documentary footage, data visualization (youth unemployment, mental health, China–EU trade flows), and a 1-bit video game—an explorable "Chimera Ruin." Its core conflict is structural: the people who make ultra-cheap goods versus the geopolitical forces that weaponize those goods as trade.

For Overgaden I propose an exhibition that brings these strands into one space: acrylic objects made with the factory, moving-image testimony, data as a material, and the game as a navigable archive—addressing Copenhagen not as a neutral site, but as a receiving port where algorithmic desire meets industrial labor.
```

**字符数：2,403**
